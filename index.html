About the “book comments project” — what, who, and why?

2023年1月3日 ~ 梁浩然 ~ Leave a comment

What is this about?

Well, just as the name suggests, this is a project on writing mathematical book reviews. Each time, we try to introduce a textbook, some notes, or what I call “a mathematical novel” (like expository texts, such as David A. Cox’s famous book Primes of the form x2+ny2, which are somewhat less formal, but usually contains more intuitions, historical remarks, etc.), share our reading experience and afterthoughts. More precisely, we aim to offer a glimpse of

  • what are covered in this book/notes;
  • what are the prerequisites;
  • specific reasons why you recommend it, for instance, in Prof. Ravi Vakil’s words: (we are not that strict, but at least it should thrill/impress you so much that you can’t help sharing with others)
    • This book changed my life.
    • This book inspired me to become a topologist. (Ideally in this case it should be a book in topology, not in real analysis…)
    • Anyone in my field who hasn’t read these notes has led an impoverished existence.
    • I wish someone had told me about this book when I was younger.
  • What should the reader be careful when he/she reads it (e.g., some part of it is a bit dense or lack of concrete examples, and what would you suggest to do);
  • further readings (if possible).

Currently, the presumed readers are mainly undergraduate students majoring and/or very interested in mathematics. It would also be a good idea to talk about great books for high school students or general audience, but at the time I don’t know much….

Who are we, and… why?

The story began about two years ago – One day when I was a senior undergraduate student, I looked back on my Bachelor’s studies and had a feeling that I went through many detours in mathematics and wasted a lot of time. I wondered why, and I thought perhaps it was partly because of lack of guidance and a view of “the big picture” (for instance, I only heard that there is a fancy area called algebraic geometry in my third year…). So, that is the moment when I started to think about writing some reviews/comments on mathematical books/notes, so that others may get a better idea of what are cool to learn and which books they could follow.

With that in mind, I organized a group of classmates and friends of mine, who really love and enjoy mathematics, to write a series of book reviews for undergraduate students studying mathematics. So far, we have about 20 (pre-)graduate students, specializing in representation theory, number theory, algebraic geometry, geometric topology, theory of operator algebras, probability theory, PDE, inverse problem, computational combinatorics, mathematical physics, etc. And we hope to cover as many areas as we can, to share as many as great topics as we know, and to create an atmosphere to discuss and learn about different mathematics together as free as possible, so please don’t hesitate to contact me if you are also interested in this project and want to join us!

A few remarks

As far as I know, there have already been quite a few materials on this topic, such as (to be completed)

  • Chicago Undergraduate Mathematics Bibliography
  • Study Plan for Mathematics Students at University of Science and Technology of China

Many thanks to these wonderful collections, and we would be really glad if more people can know about these materials from us!

We sincerely hope this project will be helpful to you, even just a tiny little bit :-).
